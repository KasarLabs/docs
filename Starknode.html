User guide

Welcome to the Starknode Plug'n'Play documentation! This guide explains the step-by-step process of setting up your very own Starknet full node and interacting with the graphical user interface

If you encounter any problems with your setup, please contact our support on Telegram.

Requirements

In this documentation, we assume that you have purchased and received your Plug'n'Play full node. If not, you can get oneIn this documentation, we assume that you have purchased and received your Plug'n'Play full node. If not, you can get one here. Stay tuned to our Telegram channel and Twitter to keep up with the latest announcements. Once you have received your node, first check if you meet the following requirements to ensure a successful setup:


What you will receive:

  • The Mew1.0 mini-computer
  • An Ethernet cable
  • A 256GB or 512GB SD card (depending on your purchase)
  • A microSD adapter
  • A micro-USB power cable

What you should already have:

  • A personal computer to setup and configure your node
  • A Starknet wallet address
  • An Ethereum L1 key (How to get your Ethereum RPC url)👇

Install Kasar GUI

Linux

You can download the binaries for your Linux distribution here.


Windows

You can download the binaries for Windows distribution here. Please unzip the folder, navigate to win-unpacked, and double-click on kasarlabs.exe to run it.


MacOS

You can download the binaries for MacOS here. Then follow these instructions:

  1. After downloading the file, unzip the folder. Open it and double click on 'kasarlabs_1.1.3.dmg'. Then drag and drop the icon into the 'Applications' folder.


  2. Open Finder and double-click on the Kasarlabs app.


  3. You should see this warning. Click on 'Cancel'


  4. Open iTerm


  5. Write the following line: cd ../../Applications and click 'enter


  6. Write the following line: xattr -c kasarlabs.app and click 'enter'


  7. Exit your finder folder and reopen it. Open the kasarlabs app in Application. And now it works!

Manage your Starknode


Once your Starknode is up and running, you may need to update it. Juno, Pathfinder and Papyrus have updates on a regular basis.

1. Remove the MicroSD card from the Starknode and plug it into your PC

Similar to the first steps of setting up your Starknode, please conenect your MicroSD card to your personal computer using the USB MicroSD flasher.


2. Detecting an update

Wait a moment to see if an update of your client is available. If there is no available update, you can simply press 'Home' on the KasarOS. If an update is available, you can click on 'Update'.

Tracking


Head to the KasarApp to track the performance of your node: https://app.kasar.io/.

The three clients available on Starknet (namely Pathfinder, Juno, and Papyrus) have an integrated tracker, allowing the user to get the live performance data of their own Full Node. This data is displayed on the dashboard on. To access this dashboard, the user must connect their account to the application. The dashboard will display insightful information such as the Average storage usage, memory usage, Minimum sync time, Transaction roots, the Parent hash, and more.

Backup your MicroSD card


Please refer to this guide for instructions on how to reformat your SD card. Be aware that this procedure should not be performed frequently. It's primarily intended for situations where the files on your SD card have been deleted, or if you're using an SD card that you've provided yourself, rather than one supplied by KasarLabs.


In this tutorial, you will learn how to accomplish the following tasks:

  1. Use the RasberryPi software to add our operating system to your SD card
  2. Connect the SD card to your7node, following the typical Starknode setup process
  3. Establish a connection between your PC and the Starknode

    4. Select 'CHOOSE OS'


  4. Select a custom Operating System and the 'kasar.img'


    5. Scroll down and select 'Use custom'


    Select 'kasar.img'


  5. Next, click on 'CHOOSE STORAGE', select the SD card and select 'write'

    6. Select 'CHOOSE STORAGE'


    Select the SD card storage option


    Once the Operating System and the Storage have been selected, click on 'WRITE'


    Click 'YES' on this pop-up message


    Wait a couple of minutes

  6. After you have finished writing, please remove the SD card from your device and then insert it back into your PC. This step is necessary because Raspberry Pi Imager will automatically eject the card once the writing process is complete



    7. Your SD card should now be renamed to 'boot'


    Launch the KasarGUI. Follow our tutorial:

  7. Choose to 'Setup my Starknode, Login or Sign-up and, select the micro SD card
  8. Next, safely eject the card, insert it into your node and, plug your node to an electrical outlet and Ethernet
  9. Once your Starknode is on, you can use the 'angryAPI' scanner, available for Windows, Mac and Linus. This software is used to find the local IP of your Starknode.
  10. In angryBirdIp, Press 'Start'. Once the scanning is complete (it should take approximately 10s to 20s), click on the 'Hostname' column and select 'Sort by Hostname'
  11. Find your device, it should have the following name 'rasberrypi.local'

    12. Tip

    Please note, your device may not always have an assigned name, for instance, it could show as 'n/a' instead of 'raspberrypi.local'. If this happens, identify all the IP addresses marked with a blue dot and proceed with step 6 and the following steps using these IP addresses. In summary, you can confirm a successful connection to your Starknode when, on MacOS or Linux, your terminal responds to the command ssh starknode@ip_address. On Windows, add the IP Address to ''Host Name (or IP address)'

  12. Copy and paste the IP address of the device, located on the first column called 'IP'
  13. Connect your Starknode through SSH

      14. For MacOS and Linus:

    1. Next, open your terminal and execute the following command by changing 'your_ip_address' with the IP you just got from 'angryBirdIp': ssh starknode@your_ip_address.
      So for example, if my IP is 192.168.1.29, I will copy+paste the following command into my terminal: ssh starknode@192.168.1.29
    2. Input the following password: !KasarOs
    3. Once the process is finished, execute this command sudo /boot/efi/src/run.sh
    4. Now, your connection should be closed
    5. Congrats! If you made it this far, you successfully reformatted your card, ran your node, and connected it to the internet ⚡️


      For Windows

    1. Download 'PuTTY'a free SSH and telnet client for Windows
    2. Open PuTTY, head to 'Host Name (or IP address)', add the IP address from your node, and click on 'Open'

      3. PuTTY interface on Windows

    3. Next, input starknode, next, the password (!KasarOs), and finally paste sudo /boot/efi/src/run.sh by right-clicking on the console

      4. Console

    4. Press ENTER
    5. Congrats, if you made it this far you reformatted your card on Windows ⚡️

    FAQ

    Setup and installation

    How do I install the node? Is there a tutorial available?

    There is a video tutorial under the “Setup your Starknode” section of our documentation here.

    What hardware and operating system requirements are needed to install the KasarGUI?

    The KasarGUI can run on any computer with a Windows, Linux, or iOS operating system and an internet connection.

    Is there a specific chain or network that should be chosen for the node?

    When setting up your Ethereum L1 key (e.g., with Alchemy), please select the Ethereum chain and the Ethereum Mainnet network.

    How do I delete or reset a node that was already set up?

    In the Kasar app, there's an option to delete a node. However, it's important to understand that deleting a node doesn't halt its operation; it simply removes the node from your visible dashboard.

    Is there a specific client recommended for the node setup?

    Selecting the client can depend on the storage size of your SD card. If you have a 256GB card you can opt for Juno, as the DB only weighs 31G, if you have a 512GB and you want to develop using the Starknode, you can opt for Pathfinder.

    Do we need to download any specific app to install the node?

    Yes, in order to complete the Starknode installation, you have to install the Kasar application. Please find the link to the download in the documentation under the ”Install KasarGUI” section.

    Sync and update

    How long does it take for the node to download the mainnet data?

    It can take up to 24 hours. However, this also depends on the number of nodes syncing with the sequencer.

    Why does the syncing process take a long time?

    The syncing process is slowed down by all the nodes syncing from the same sequencer while the state is growing. This will be unlocked really soon with future updates.

    Is it normal for the node to be stuck on a certain step (0%, 64%, unzip mainnet etc.)?

    It is typical for downloading mainnet to take some time and unzipping the mainnet will also require waiting for about 24 hours for Pathfinder and 1 hour for Juno, though this duration may vary depending on your internet connection. If your connection speed is slow, the download time for the mainnet could be longer.

    How to handle if a node has stopped syncing?

    It is normal there is a large number of full nodes wanting to sync all at once.

    What happens if the node is unplugged during the syncing process?

    Your Starknode will keep syncing back from where it was left, even if you change the router.

    Can the node use other providers like Infura or Alchemy as an RPC?

    You can use Infura, blast, Chainstack, or any other RPC provider.


    Storage and performance

    How much storage is needed for the first sync and future syncs?

    For the first sync, Juno weighs 31G and Pathfinder 289G. Future syncs will depend on the growth of the network.

    What happens when the blockchain becomes larger than the available storage?

    It will take some time until you reach 100% but whenever it's the case you should upgrade it or opt for a light client solution. If you look at Juno the entirety of their state is 31G against 289G for Pathfinder. Pathfinder will soon work on that. We are also working on Deoxys, our own client implementation at KasarLabs. This one will sync way faster because it integrates p2p and other performance optimizations.

    Is the node consuming too much storage space (1% per day)?

    With the latest version of Juno and its snapshot, you will have significantly more available storage capacity.

    How to handle storage issues, like reformatting an SD card or using a new SD card?

    It is common for Windows to offer SD card reformatting. We ship the SD cards already flashed with our OS, so if you reformat it or decide to use another one, you will not have the Kasar OS installed. There is a step-by-step guide on how to reformat the Sd card in the documentation under the “Backup your SD card” section here.


    Connectivity and hardware

    What do the various LED light indicators mean?

    The node is considered to be functioning properly when specific lights are displayed. The power section of the node should have a steady red light and a blinking green light. In the Ethernet area, there should be a blinking green light and a blinking yellow light. If you do not observe these blinking lights, please disconnect and reconnect your Starknode. It is recommended to connect your Starknode directly to a wall outlet for the most reliable power source. If any of the lights do not meet the mentioned requirements, the node will not sync.

    What happens if there's an Internet outage or if the node has to be unplugged?

    The syncing will stop at the last block and re-sync back from it when you plug it or get connected again.

    Does the node need to be connected to the computer as well as the router?

    Only your router and power cable should be connected to your Starknode.

    Can any USB plug be used to power the device?

    Use the microUSB port to your Starknode. Please use a wall outlet for the best power source.


    Functionality and utility

    Can the node be used to improve transaction speed?

    No, running a Starknode will not increase transaction speed.

    Is it recommended to create a new wallet for the node?

    You can use any L2 wallet and create a new one just for the Starknode.

    What is the role of the Starknet address during configuration?

    This is in the provision for any consensus protocol integration.


    Purchase

    Is it safe to purchase on secondary markets?

    There is no secondary market. It is not recommended to buy any Starknode elsewhere than https://kasar.io.

    Rewards and incentives

    Will Starknet reward node runners?

    There is currently no consensus on Starknet announced by the Foundation, so running your Starknode will not currently deliver rewards.


    Suppot and troubleshooting

    How to handle issues with specific steps during the node setup process?

    If you strictly follow each step during the node setup, you shouldn't encounter any problems. If you're stuck at the 'Node Created' stage, have you completed the SSH step? Please remember to read the entire documentation.


    General

    Are there any limitations or restrictions on running multiple nodes or devices from the same IP address?

    There is no limit to the number of nodes you can run on the same network. However, it is important to note that when multiple nodes are active, they may sync at a slower rate due to them all accessing a single sequencer.

    How much electricity and data does the node consume?

    It depends on your country but It consumes a low amount of electricity, ranging from around 1.5 to 3.5 watts under normal usage conditions.

    Should the node be running 24/7 or only when using the network?

    When you disconnect it, it will not sync so you need to sync before use in the live network, but you can disconnect and continue to sync where you stopped before you reconnect it.

    At what stage of the setup do I become a live full node?

    From the moment you are syncing, you become a Starknet full node. In other words, you do not need to be fully syncing to be a Starknet full node.
